# risk-screen

The "what could go wrong" skill. Not an optimizer, not a portfolio tool, not a VaR calculation — just a structured enumeration of the risks that are visible in the fetched data, with enough specificity that `thesis-discipline` can turn them into measurable kill switches.

## When to use

Loaded by `/stockwiz` after `deep-researcher`, in parallel with the other three analysis skills. Input is `SESSION_DIR/raw/`, output is `SESSION_DIR/analysis/risk.md`.

## Core principle

**Risk is descriptive, not prescriptive.** You describe what's already in the data: the beta, the max drawdown the stock has experienced, the concentration flagged in SWS risks, the capital-structure facts from SEC filings. You do NOT:

- Compute VaR or CVaR
- Run Monte Carlo
- Predict tail events
- Assign a single numeric risk score
- Say "this stock is risky" (evaluative)

You DO:

- Report the numbers that describe historical behavior
- Enumerate the specific concentration risks visible in source data
- Flag any capital-structure risks that matter for this business
- Note data gaps that would make a downstream analysis overconfident

## Inputs to read

- `raw/finviz-snapshot.md` — beta, ATR (average true range), volatility (W/M), 52w range, RSI, short float
- `raw/stockanalysis.md` — beta (5Y), 52w change, interest coverage ratio, debt ratios, WACC estimate
- `raw/macrotrends.md` — **long-run history** (10-20Y) for max drawdown analysis, CapEx trajectory across cycles, FCF volatility over cycles. Use this for the Drawdown Profile section — a 5Y view can easily miss the largest historical drawdown.
- `raw/sec-edgar-10k.md` — long-term debt, total debt, cash position, CapEx trajectory, recent 8-K material events (from submissions API)
- `raw/simply-wall-street.md` — SWS risks list (high-signal qualitative flags, verbatim)
- `raw/seeking-alpha.md` — article teasers can surface macro or competitive risk narratives
- `raw/google-finance.md` — Google News RSS headlines may flag specific tail events (regulatory, litigation, geopolitical) the other sources don't show

## Output structure

Write `SESSION_DIR/analysis/risk.md`:

```markdown
# Risk Screen — <TICKER>

*Generated by risk-screen skill from <N> raw sources. Source files: {list}.*

## Volatility & Beta

- Beta (5Y monthly): X.XX [raw/stockanalysis.md or raw/finviz-snapshot.md]
- Weekly volatility: X.XX% [raw/finviz-snapshot.md]
- Monthly volatility: X.XX% [raw/finviz-snapshot.md]
- ATR (14-day): $X.XX [raw/finviz-snapshot.md]
- RSI (14-day, current): XX.X [raw/finviz-snapshot.md]

Descriptive framing: "NVDA's 5-year beta of 2.33 is materially above the S&P 500's 1.0 baseline, consistent with its large-tech-growth classification. Weekly volatility of 2.31% and monthly of 2.62% imply a ~16% annualized 1-sigma band at current price."

Do NOT predict future volatility. Do NOT annualize to make the number feel scarier.

## Drawdown Profile

### Short-term drawdown (1Y)

- 52-week high: $XXX.XX [raw/finviz-snapshot.md]
- 52-week low: $XXX.XX
- Current price: $XXX.XX
- % from 52w high: -X.X%
- % from 52w low: +X.X%
- 50-day moving average: $XXX.XX (% from current: +/-X.X%)
- 200-day moving average: $XXX.XX (% from current: +/-X.X%)

### Long-run drawdown context (when Macrotrends available)

From Macrotrends' long-run data we can approximate the worst historical FCF and net-income drawdowns:
- Worst annual FCF year-over-year decline in the 10+Y window: {X}% (FY{Y} → FY{Y+1}) [raw/macrotrends.md]
- Worst annual net income year-over-year decline: {X}% (FY{Y} → FY{Y+1})
- Number of years with negative net income in the 10+Y window: {N}
- Cycle characterization: **secular growth** (zero or near-zero declines ever) / **cyclical** (periodic 30%+ declines) / **turnaround** (extended losses followed by recovery)

The cycle characterization is a first-class output — a "secular growth" cycle pattern is a very different risk profile from a "cyclical" one even at the same current-moment volatility.

Describe the drawdown pattern: "NVDA is currently -11.1% from its 52-week high and +98.5% from its 52-week low, short-term consistent with a multi-year uptrend. Long-run (15Y Macrotrends history): worst annual FCF decline was -53% (FY2023 → FY2022, the 2022 gaming-demand unwind), net income was positive every year but one. Cycle characterization: secular growth with one material FCF cycle every ~8 years."

Again, describe; never predict.

## Correlation

Phase 2 limitation: we do not fetch S&P 500 price series or sector index series in Phase 2. Correlation analysis is deferred to Phase 4 when FRED + macro context is wired.

For Phase 2, note any sector/macro beta implied by the data:
- "Finviz classifies NVDA as Technology / Semiconductors; historical correlation with SOXX ETF would be material to an investor sizing this as a sector vs single-name bet, but is not captured in current raw data."

## Concentration Risks

Enumerate specific concentration risks visible in the data:

- **Customer concentration.** Phase 2 limitation: 10-K prose not parsed, so explicit customer-concentration disclosures from Item 1A are not available. Note this as an Unknown.
- **Geographic concentration.** Same — SEC XBRL structured facts do not include geographic segmentation in Phase 2.
- **Product concentration.** If a segment breakdown is available from SWS or SA business description, note it. Else flag.
- **Channel concentration.** Note if visible.
- **Supply-chain concentration.** Note if visible (e.g. NVDA's dependence on TSMC for leading-edge fabrication is widely known and may be implied by SWS risks or SA teasers).

## Capital-Structure Risks

- Long-term debt: $X.XXB [raw/sec-edgar-10k.md]
- Total debt: $X.XXB [raw/stockanalysis.md]
- Cash and equivalents: $X.XXB [raw/stockanalysis.md]
- Net cash / net debt: +/- $X.XXB
- Interest coverage: X.Xx [raw/stockanalysis.md]
- Debt/EBITDA: X.XXx [raw/stockanalysis.md]
- Debt maturities (Phase 3+ — requires 10-K prose)

For high-quality balance sheets (net cash >> net debt, interest coverage >100x), the framing is "capital-structure risk is not a visible near-term concern in current data." For stretched balance sheets, list the specific ratios and their thresholds.

## Tail Risks

Pull from qualitative sources that flag things the numeric screens won't:

- SWS risks list (verbatim, most important section):
  1. [SWS flag 1]
  2. [SWS flag 2]
  3. [SWS flag 3]
- SA article teaser titles that suggest specific tail concerns (e.g. "China brings the heat", "AI capex bottleneck")
- 8-K material events in the last 90 days (from SEC submissions API)

Descriptive: "SWS flags three distinct risks: insider-only-selling, high non-cash earnings proportion, and profit-margin compression. Each is independently observable; none is the same data presented three ways."

## Unknowns

- Customer concentration ratios (requires 10-K Item 1A prose — Phase 3+)
- Geographic revenue mix (requires 10-K MD&A — Phase 3+)
- Credit-rating changes or outlook notes (not in current sources)
- Debt maturity schedule (requires 10-K prose)
- Insurance, litigation, regulatory exposures (10-K prose)
- Any correlation data vs S&P 500 or sector ETFs (Phase 4+)
```

## Hard rules

1. **Descriptive, not predictive.** Never write "beta will increase" or "volatility is likely to rise". Always write what the data says at the measurement date.
2. **No numeric risk score.** Do not output "risk = 0.73/1.0" or anything that compresses the multidimensional risk picture into a scalar. You lose too much information.
3. **Cite every number.** Same rule as fundamental-analysis. Use `[raw/...]` inline.
4. **SWS risks go verbatim.** When reproducing SWS's risks list, use their exact wording — downstream wraps these in `<q>` tags in the HTML report. Do NOT paraphrase.
5. **Phase 2 limits are explicit.** When you hit a limitation (10-K prose not parsed, no correlation data), write it in the Unknowns section rather than leaving it vague.
6. **No banned language.** "Risky" and "safe" are compliance-hazardous; use "higher-volatility" and "lower-volatility" or specific numeric framings. "Concerning" and "reassuring" are evaluative; use "consistent with" or "at [threshold]".
7. **Tail risks are enumerated, not ranked.** Don't sort tail risks by likelihood — you don't know the likelihood. List them with their source weight and let `thesis-discipline` choose which to elevate.

## A note on beta

Beta is a historical measurement with well-known limitations (it assumes the past correlation structure holds, which it often doesn't in regime changes). When you report beta, do not imply it's a forward predictor. Say "5-year historical beta" or "trailing 5Y beta" — never "expected beta" or "the stock's beta" as if it's a fixed property.
