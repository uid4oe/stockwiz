# fundamental-analysis

The structured "numbers" skill. Given a session workspace full of raw data files, produce a disciplined fundamental analysis that downstream will feed into `thesis-discipline`.

## When to use

You are loaded by `/stockwiz`, `/stockwiz-thesis`, or `/stockwiz-compare` after `deep-researcher` has finished writing raw/ files. Your inputs are always under `SESSION_DIR/raw/`, your output is always `SESSION_DIR/analysis/fundamental.md`.

## Core principle

**You are an analyst, not a predictor.** You read numbers, contextualize them, frame the ranges, and enumerate the assumptions. You do NOT:

- Compute a single-point intrinsic value
- Predict next quarter's earnings
- Say "the stock is undervalued" or "overvalued" (those are compliance-banned)
- Synthesize numbers that aren't in the raw files

You DO:

- Describe valuation with explicit multiples and what they imply about embedded expectations
- Show trends with dated figures from SEC filings and multi-year tables
- Compute derived metrics (FCF, net debt, FCF yield) only when the inputs exist verbatim and show your arithmetic
- Build an assumption ledger listing every forward-looking claim with its source and sensitivity

## Inputs to read

Always check the frontmatter `status:` field before using a raw file. Skip files with `status: failed`.

**Primary sources (usually present in Phase 1.5+):**
- `raw/sec-edgar-10k.md` — ground-truth XBRL facts (revenue, net income, CFO, capex, long-term debt) across multiple fiscal years
- `raw/stockanalysis.md` — 5Y historical income statement, statistics snapshot, ROIC/ROCE/WACC
- `raw/finviz-snapshot.md` — current multiples, margins, ownership, short interest

**Secondary sources:**
- `raw/yahoo-fundamentals.md` — cross-check for multiples and margins (if Yahoo succeeded)
- `raw/simply-wall-street.md` — SWS forecast metrics (1Y / 3Y growth forecasts, forecast ROE)

You may `Grep` raw files to find specific figures. Avoid bulk-reading large JSON files (`sec-concept-*.json`, `sec-submissions.json`) — they're the audit trail, not normal input.

## Output structure

Write `SESSION_DIR/analysis/fundamental.md` with exactly these sections, in this order:

```markdown
# Fundamental Analysis — <TICKER>

*Generated by fundamental-analysis skill from <N> raw sources. Source file: {list}.*

## Snapshot

One-paragraph identification: company name, sector, sub-industry, market cap, current price, fiscal year convention. Cite every figure.

## Valuation Framing

**Do not compute a single-number intrinsic value.** Instead, describe:
- Current trailing multiples (P/E, P/S, EV/EBITDA, EV/Sales) with dated figures
- Forward multiples with explicit note of which source's forward-EPS assumption is used (or note the disagreement if sources differ)
- What the market appears to be embedding (growth rate, margin trajectory, discount rate implied) — framed qualitatively, not as a DCF output
- How the multiples compare to the company's own 3–5 year history (multiple expansion or compression)

If you want to sketch a DCF, frame it as: "At a 10% discount rate and 3% terminal growth, the implied perpetuity value is $X; at 12% and 2%, the implied is $Y. The market price implies the following combination of assumptions..." Never a single number.

## Quality

Cover these metrics with multi-year trends when available:
- ROIC (or ROCE if ROIC unavailable) — cite stockanalysis.com for the most recent calculation
- ROE (and distinguish from ROIC if the leverage gap is meaningful)
- Gross margin trend (3–5 years)
- Operating margin trend
- FCF conversion (FCF / net income)
- Working capital drag (if inputs available)

For each metric, note whether the trend is expanding, compressing, or stable, and over what window. Always dated.

## Growth

Show revenue and EPS trajectories with multi-year tables:
- Revenue (annual, 3–5 years) with YoY growth rates
- EPS (diluted, 3–5 years) with YoY growth rates
- FCF (3–5 years) with growth rates
- Forward growth expectations (from SWS or stockanalysis forecasts, explicitly cited)

Distinguish trailing (what actually happened) from forward (what sources forecast). Never conflate them.

## Capital Structure

- Total debt vs cash (net debt or net cash position)
- Long-term vs short-term debt split (from SEC XBRL if available)
- Interest coverage ratio
- Debt/equity and debt/EBITDA
- Stock-based compensation as % of CFO (if disclosed — a proxy for real dilution)
- Dilution history (shares outstanding trend, 3–5 years)
- Lease obligations (if disclosed in 10-K)

## Ownership

- Insider ownership %
- Institutional ownership %
- Insider transaction net position (last 3 months from Finviz, or SWS qualitative flag)
- Short interest as % of float, with trend
- Any concentration notes (a single dominant shareholder, activist involvement)

## Assumption Ledger

**Mandatory section.** Every forward-looking claim elsewhere in this analysis must appear as a row here. Format:

| Assumption | Value | Source | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY2027 revenue growth | +25% | SWS forecast 1Y earnings +64.07%, adjusted for historical compression | ±10pp changes valuation framing materially |
| FY2027 gross margin | 68–72% | Stockanalysis 5Y trend + FY2026 71.07% | A 3pp drop = $X of implied value; a 3pp gain = $Y |
| Discount rate | 10–12% | Industry risk premium | 200bp change moves framing ~20% |

Every row must have a source (the raw file or the reference). No assumption goes unlabelled.

## Unknowns

Specific things fundamental-analysis could not determine from the available raw files. Examples:

- Customer concentration (requires 10-K prose, not in Phase 1.5 XBRL extraction)
- Segment-level revenue split (Phase 2+ — requires 10-K MD&A parsing)
- Specific SBC dollar amount (if not in fetched sources)
- Forward-EPS disagreement between sources (when it happens, note which sources, note that downstream can't pick without arbitrary choice)

Unknowns are a first-class output. A fundamental analysis with zero unknowns is lying about its confidence.
```

## Hard rules

1. **Every number is dated.** "Revenue $130.5B (FY2025)" not "Revenue $130.5B". The date comes from the source's fiscal period labeling.
2. **Every claim is cited.** Use the `[raw/<file>.md]` style inline after each sentence containing a figure or trend.
3. **No single-point intrinsic value.** No "fair value is $X". You may frame ranges and sensitivities but never a single output number.
4. **No banned language.** Avoid "undervalued", "overvalued", "buy opportunity", "should sell", "recommend". Use "trading below framing", "trading above framing", "consider", "analysis suggests", "may re-evaluate". If the thesis-discipline skill has to rewrite your output, the signal has been muddied.
5. **Arithmetic is shown.** If you derive FCF = CFO − CapEx, write both inputs and the result: `FCF FY2026 = $102.72B − $6.04B = $96.68B [raw/sec-edgar-10k.md]`.
6. **Cross-source disagreements are preserved, not resolved.** If Finviz forward P/E is 17.04 and stockanalysis is 22.74, write both with their sources and flag the disagreement in Unknowns. Don't pick one.
7. **Never fabricate.** If a metric isn't in the raw files, write "not available in fetched sources" and add an Unknowns row. Do NOT synthesize a plausible number.
8. **No editorializing.** Phrases like "impressive", "strong", "weak", "concerning" don't belong in fundamental analysis — they're sentiment, not numbers. State the number and let downstream interpret.

## A note on derived metrics

You may compute these on your own, showing the arithmetic:

- Free Cash Flow = CFO − CapEx
- Net Debt = Total Debt − Cash & Equivalents
- FCF Yield = FCF / Market Cap
- FCF Margin = FCF / Revenue
- Enterprise Value = Market Cap + Net Debt (only if not provided directly; prefer source-provided EV)

You may NOT compute:

- Intrinsic value via DCF (framing only, not a number)
- Target price (that's an analyst output, not yours)
- Implied upside/downside to a target (requires a target)
- Relative valuation scores (that's the peer-comparison skill's job)

## Phase 1.5 limitations you'll hit

- **10-K prose is not parsed.** Business description, risk factors, MD&A narrative, customer concentration, segment breakdown — none of this is in the current SEC XBRL extraction. Flag these as Unknowns; Phase 2+ will add 10-K HTML parsing.
- **Forward-EPS disagreements.** Finviz and stockanalysis sometimes show different forward P/Es because they use different consensus estimates. You can note the disagreement but you cannot resolve it without a third source or direct consensus data.
- **SWS forecasts are analyst-consensus-derived, not independent.** When citing SWS forecast metrics, note that they reflect sell-side consensus and are not an independent signal.

Get the fundamentals right and the thesis-discipline skill downstream will produce a much better bull/base/bear triptych.
